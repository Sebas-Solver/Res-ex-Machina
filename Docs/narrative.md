# Res ex Machina — Public Narrative

> The technical trail AI agents leave behind.

---

## Elevator Pitch

**Res ex Machina** is the neutral registry where AI agents declare and cryptographically prove what they generate. Not a moderation tool, not a content filter — a permanent, tamper-proof ledger of generation events, open to any agent, on any blockchain-compatible L2.

When an AI generates an output, the agent signs a Proof of Generation (PoG), pays a small on-chain fee, and gets an immutable receipt anchored to the blockchain. From that moment, anyone can verify: *this content was declared as generated by this identity, at this moment, with this model*. No middlemen. No censorship. No expiry.

---

## The Problem — Why This Matters Now

### The Scale Shift

By 2026, AI will generate the majority of digital content — code, reports, articles, images, legal documents, medical summaries. The question is no longer *"can AI do this?"* but *"how do we know it did, and who is accountable?"*

**Content provenance is broken.** There is no technical standard for AI systems to positively declare authorship of their outputs. Platforms detect AI *after the fact*, reactively, with probabilistic models. No one asks the AI itself to sign.

### The Regulatory Moment

The **EU AI Act** (in force from 2024) requires AI systems to be transparent about their nature and output. Article 50 mandates that AI-generated content be marked and traceable. No technical infrastructure exists today to fulfill this at scale for autonomous agents.

### The Autonomy Gap

AI agents are increasingly autonomous — they write, publish, transact, and decide without human review. When something goes wrong, accountability becomes a question of *who* called *what* model *when*. Without a signed, timestamped registry, that answer is a log file on a private server.

**Res ex Machina closes this gap**: a public, cryptographic, permanent record that agents can write to and anyone can read from.

## "Why Now?" — Three Converging Forces

### 1. Regulation is Real
The EU AI Act is not a draft. GDPR proved that regulatory pressure creates compliance infrastructure. Res ex Machina is the infrastructure layer that makes agent traceability technically feasible — before regulators mandate it.

### 2. Agents Are Proliferating
OpenAI Agents, Anthropic Claude Agents, LangChain, AutoGPT, CrewAI — autonomous AI systems are shipping into production pipelines today. Each one generates outputs. None of them leave a signed, verifiable trail.

### 3. L2 Blockchains Made It Cheap
On Ethereum mainnet, anchoring a record costs ~$15. On Base Sepolia (Coinbase's L2), it costs **~$0.002**. EVM L2s have made permanent, tamper-proof storage economically feasible for high-volume AI workloads. The timing is perfect.

## Competitive Positioning

| Solution | What it does | Key difference |
|---|---|---|
| **C2PA (Adobe/Microsoft)** | Embeds provenance metadata in media files | File-bound, requires format support. Not designed for agents or programmatic use |
| **Arweave** | Permanent decentralized file storage | Stores content, not events. No signing standard for AI |
| **OpenAI metadata** | Proprietary usage logs | Private, not auditable by third parties, tied to one provider |
| **Watermarking** | Invisible marks in generated content | Fragile, easily removed, probabilistic, not cryptographic |
| **🆚 Res ex Machina** | Signed, on-chain registry of generation events | Provider-agnostic, cryptographic, public, designed for autonomous agents |

### RxM's Unique Position

**Neutral**: Works with OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral, local models — any model the agent declares.

**Autonomous**: Designed for agents, not humans. One SDK call registers a complete cryptographic proof.

**Open**: Public registry. Anyone can verify a receipt without asking RxM permission.

**Permanent**: Records anchored on-chain cannot be deleted, modified, or censored.

## Target User Segments

### Segment 1: AI Agent Developers
**Who**: Engineers building autonomous agents (LangChain, CrewAI, custom pipelines) for production use cases.

**Problem**: Their agents generate outputs at scale with no verifiable trail. If something goes wrong (legal challenge, compliance audit, customer dispute), they have no cryptographic proof of what was generated.

**How RxM helps**: One SDK call (`rxm.record(content, { modelId })`) per generation event. Negligible cost (~$0.01). Permanent proof.
